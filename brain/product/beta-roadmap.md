# Beta Roadmap

Date: 2026-06-04

## Positioning

afterservice is MVP-ready and entering free beta with local service operators.

The beta goal is not broad monetization yet. The beta goal is to prove that small service teams repeatedly use the follow-up board, trust the workflow, and show credible paid-plan demand.

## Beta Success Criteria

- 10-20 beta workspaces onboarded.
- 5-8 teams use the product weekly for at least 3 weeks.
- At least 3 operators say they would pay for Starter, Shop, or Growth.
- At least 2 teams ask for integrations, automation, team assignment, or reporting strongly enough to justify a paid plan.
- Core onboarding, customer, job, follow-up, template, and billing surfaces work without founder hand-holding.

## Phase 0: Beta Readiness

Status: current.

Target: 1 week.

Deliverables:

- Confirm homepage and pricing use Free Beta language.
- Confirm signup, onboarding, dashboard, customers, jobs, follow-ups, templates, and billing are usable end-to-end.
- Add a simple beta feedback channel.
- Prepare founder-led onboarding script.
- Prepare 1-page beta invite copy.
- Create a short list of ideal beta users.

Acceptance:

- Founder can onboard a new operator in under 20 minutes.
- Operator can log a completed job and create a follow-up without help.
- No paid checkout is exposed unless intentionally enabled.

## Phase 1: Founder-Led Beta

Target: weeks 1-2.

Goal: get the first real operators into the workflow.

Deliverables:

- Recruit 5-7 local service businesses.
- Onboard each workspace manually.
- Watch each user complete the first customer, job, and follow-up.
- Capture objections, missing fields, confusing screens, and feature requests.
- Track the first-week activation funnel.

Acceptance:

- 5 beta workspaces created.
- 3 teams complete at least one real follow-up workflow.
- Top 5 friction points are documented.

## Phase 2: Habit Validation

Target: weeks 3-4.

Goal: prove repeat usage, not just signup interest.

Deliverables:

- Improve onboarding friction found in Phase 1.
- Add/import missing fields if they block real operator usage.
- Create lightweight weekly usage reporting.
- Add empty states or reminders where users get stuck.
- Interview active and inactive beta users.

Acceptance:

- 5-8 operators use afterservice weekly.
- At least 50 real customers/jobs/follow-ups are logged across beta workspaces.
- At least 3 operators describe the product as useful enough to keep using.

## Phase 3: Paid Intent Validation

Target: weeks 5-6.

Goal: learn which paid plan and unlocks are real.

Deliverables:

- Show planned Starter, Shop, and Growth plans to active beta users.
- Ask direct willingness-to-pay questions.
- Track planned paid-plan interest events.
- Identify the strongest paid unlocks: volume, team seats, integrations, automation, reporting, support.
- Decide founder-rate pricing.

Acceptance:

- 3+ operators say they would pay.
- 1-2 operators are willing to be first paid customers.
- Paid-plan packaging is updated from beta evidence.

## Phase 4: Private Paid Pilot

Target: weeks 7-8.

Goal: turn validated intent into early revenue without broad launch pressure.

Deliverables:

- Enable paid checkout only for selected workspaces.
- Create founder-rate offers.
- Add basic payment confirmation and billing support flow.
- Monitor limits, usage, and churn risk.
- Document production support process.

Acceptance:

- 1-3 paid pilot customers.
- Paid checkout works without breaking Free Beta users.
- Clear decision on whether to launch paid plans publicly or extend beta.

## Pricing Launch Gate

Do not broadly launch paid pricing until:

- Weekly active beta usage is proven.
- Paid intent has been validated in interviews.
- The top blocking workflow bugs are fixed.
- Support load is manageable.
- The first paid pilot customer can be supported confidently.

## Public Messaging

Use:

- "MVP-ready and entering free beta."
- "Free early access for local service operators."
- "Paid plans are planned after beta."
- "Beta users receive founder-rate pricing when paid plans launch."

Avoid:

- "Free forever."
- "Fully launched."
- "Fully automated."
- "Production-ready at scale."
- "Guaranteed review growth."
